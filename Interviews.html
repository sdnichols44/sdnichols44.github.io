Interviews Compiler

Build your Interview here! Unlike most other compilers, there are multiple compilers on this page, one for each piece of the Interview. When you compile the question, the fields that are required to be identical for all questions are automatically filled for you, to prevent mismatches. However, the fields are left available to you if for some reason you wish to change them to something else, such as in the event you only want to make a new answer set for an existing question.

Unlike most other sections, but similar to Dilemmas, Interviews.txt has multiple separate sections that all fit together like a puzzle. The question will set a reference that is then used for the answers to match them together. The answers can then also optionally connect to other questions (both those that already exist in Interviews.txt and those that you are yet to create), or to the conclusion sections already supplied in Interviews.txt. This allows you to both insert your interview question/answers into the chain, or have it always be the final question.

Your interview must have a question, and can have between 1 to 4 answers inclusively. It is possible to utilise existing asnwers with your question, but it is advisable to create bespoke answers to your questions. As with Dilemmas, if your sections are not properly linked, you will encounter a range of issues that can be from your question never spawning, questions with no answers attached (which prevents progression), all the way up to and including crashing the game.
